Space Exploration Timeline

Major milestones from the first satellite to modern deep-space missions.

1957 — Sputnik 1

USSR launches the first artificial satellite, starting the Space Age.

1961 — Yuri Gagarin

First human to orbit Earth aboard Vostok 1.

1969 — Apollo 11

Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin land on the Moon.

1971 — Salyut 1

World’s first space station is launched.

1977 — Voyager 1

Probe begins its grand tour; later becomes the most distant human-made object.

1990 — Hubble Space Telescope

Launch opens a new era of astronomy with stunning deep-space images.

1998 — International Space Station

Assembly of the ISS begins, enabling long-term microgravity research.

2012 — Curiosity Rover

NASA’s rover lands on Mars to study climate and geology.

2021 — James Webb Space Telescope

Launch of the most powerful space telescope to date.
